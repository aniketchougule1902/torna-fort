Architecture & features

Zunjar Machi

Zunjar Machi ‚Äì the largest plateau. It has two small temples and offers a 360¬∞ view. It was used to keep cattle and as a camping ground for soldiers.

Budhla Machi

Budhla Machi ‚Äì named after 'Budhla' (old man). It has a large cave and a water cistern that never dries. Ruins of an ancient temple are also here.

Torana gateways

Massive stone gates ‚Äì the main entrance has a clever design with sharp turns to slow down enemy elephants and cavalry.

Water reservoirs

More than 20 rock-cut cisterns ‚Äì some still hold water. The engineering ensured year-round supply for the garrison.

The fort also has several caves, probably used by monks or as storage. The walls are built with huge interlocking stones ‚Äì no mortar ‚Äì a hallmark of ancient Indian hill forts.

Key to the Maratha Empire

"The first victory that ignited the flame of Swarajya."

Why was Torna so important? In simple words ‚Äì it gave Shivaji Maharaj his first big success and the money to build a kingdom. The treasure from Torna helped:

  • Build a strong army of loyal soldiers.
  • Construct and repair other forts like Rajgad.
  • Show the world that the Marathas could challenge big powers like the Adilshahi and Mughals.

After Torna, many other forts were captured, and the Maratha flag flew high. Even today, Torna is a symbol of courage and the beginning of an empire.
